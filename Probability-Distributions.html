1.Bernoulli distribution {X BERN(p)}

The Bernoulli distribution is the probability distribution of a discrete random variable that has only two possible outcomes, such as success or failure.

#The probability of success(getting a head) in one toss of a fair coin
dbinom(x = 1, size = 1, prob = 0.5)
## [1] 0.5
#The probability of getting a tail
dbinom(x = 0, size = 1, prob = 0.5)
## [1] 0.5
#Note that size(n) = 1 for a Bernoulli trial
#The probability of getting a tail in one toss of a bias coin, with p = 0.3
dbinom(x = 0, size = 1, prob = 0.3)
## [1] 0.7

2.Binomial distribution {X BIN(n, p)}; mean = np, var = np(1-p)

The binomial distribution is the distribution of successes in n number of trials involving binary discrete random variables.

#Rolling a fair die example
#What is the probability of getting a 4 when you roll a die 8 times?
dbinom(x = 4, size = 8, prob = (1/6))
## [1] 0.02604762
#The probability of getting face 5 showing up
dbinom(x = 5, size = 8, prob = (1/6))
## [1] 0.004167619
#Full pmf for the die 
Die_pmf <- dbinom(x = 0:8, size = 8, prob = (1/6))
Die_pmf
## [1] 2.325680e-01 3.721089e-01 2.604762e-01 1.041905e-01 2.604762e-02
## [6] 4.167619e-03 4.167619e-04 2.381497e-05 5.953742e-07
#Confirm if sum adds upto 1
sum(Die_pmf)
## [1] 1
#Round off the probabilities to 4sf
round(Die_pmf,4)
## [1] 0.2326 0.3721 0.2605 0.1042 0.0260 0.0042 0.0004 0.0000 0.0000
#Mean and variance
np <- (8*(1/6))
np
## [1] 1.333333
npq <- (8*(1/6)*(5/6))
npq
## [1] 1.111111
#Plot the probability mass function
barplot(Die_pmf, names.arg=0:8, space=0, xlab="x", ylab="Pr(X = x)")
